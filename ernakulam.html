Ernakulam

Ernakulam, is one of the 14 districts in the Indian state of Kerala, that takes its name from the eponymous city division in Kochi.
Places to visit in Ernakulam are :

Santa Cruz Cathedral Basilica Fort Kochi

The Santa Cruz Cathedral Basilica at Fort Kochi, Kochi, is one of the nine basilicas in Kerala. Counted as one of the heritage edifices of Kerala, this church is one of the finest and most impressive churches in India and visited by tourists the whole year round.

Willingdon Island

Man-made Willingdon Island, at the heart of Cochin Port, is a busy hub for commercial shipping and cruise liners. It’s a popular destination for ferry trips from the mainland, and a departure point for backwater boat tours. At the port’s quaint Maritime Heritage Museum, rare photographs and nautical relics trace local seafaring history. The quieter, green areas around the island are home to several upmarket hotels.

Marine Drive

Marine Drive is a buzzing waterfront district known for the Marine Walkway, popular for evening strolls, and leafy Subhash Bose Park. The modern Rainbow and Kettuvallam bridges offer backwater views, and cruises leave from jetties by the water. Affluent homes and offices fill the area, and shops along Broadway sell jewelry, spices, and local fabrics. Dining ranges from upscale hotel restaurants to no-frills cafes.

Jew Town

Historic Jew Town, the heart of the once-thriving Cochin Jewish community, is known for its old-world charm and 16th-century Paradesi Synagogue. Quaint shops around Synagogue Lane and Jew Town Road sell antiques, carvings, and vintage collectibles, along with Keralan crafts and aromatic spices. Laid-back outdoor cafes and artsy eateries, some in heritage buildings, serve local specialties and Western fare.

Wonderla Amusement Park

Wonderla is the largest chain of amusement parks in India. It is owned and operated by Wonderla Holidays Limited.
